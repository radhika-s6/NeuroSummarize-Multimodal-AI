NeuroSummarize, a multimodal AI system that simplifies the interpretation of complex neuroimaging reports by generating summaries for clinicians and laypeople!

It addresses a real challenge in clinical neuroscience, turning complex, unstructured data into insights that improve understanding, efficiency, and patient communication.

It integrates:

🧩 OCR and Computer Vision to interpret diverse document formats like typed, scanned, and handwritten brain scan reports

🤖 Large Language Models for factual summarisation and information extraction

🧠 3D Brain Mapping for visual interpretability of affected regions

📊 Conversion of findings into structured, easy-to-understand outputs


Key Achievements: 

✔️ Achieved 87% factual accuracy in summarising and mapping

✔️ Dual-perspective summaries (technical + patient-friendly)

✔️ GDPR-compliant and explainable AI design

This project represents months of work combining computer vision, natural language processing, and ethical AI principles. It is designed to bridge clinical data and human understanding by generating both technical and layperson-friendly summaries, supporting faster, clearer decision-making and improving communication and accessibility in healthcare.
